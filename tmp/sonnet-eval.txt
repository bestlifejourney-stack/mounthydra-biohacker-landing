I'll analyze this stem cell/exosome company's website content for FDA compliance issues. Based on the claims you've provided, there are several significant regulatory concerns.

## HIGH SEVERITY ISSUES

### 1. **Drug Claims While Marketing as "Cosmetic/Research Only"**
**Problematic Language:**
- "promoting regeneration, reducing inflammation, and modulating the immune system"
- "molecules for healing and immune support"
- "Improved clinical performance and outcomes"
- "faster recovery and better results"

**Issue:** These are clear drug claims (treating inflammation, affecting immune function, promoting healing) that trigger FDA drug regulations, contradicting the "cosmetic use" and "research use only" disclaimers.

**Risk:** Products making drug claims are regulated as drugs regardless of labeling.

**Recommended Fix:** Remove all therapeutic, healing, and medical outcome claims OR register as investigational drugs.

### 2. **Misleading FDA Registration Claims**
**Problematic Language:**
- "FDA (with Type II Drug Master File) Certified cell line"
- "Registered with an FDA Master File"

**Issue:** FDA doesn't "certify" cell lines. Drug Master Files are confidential submissions that don't constitute FDA approval or certification.

**Risk:** Misrepresenting FDA relationship is a federal offense.

**Recommended Fix:** Change to "Cell line information filed with FDA Drug Master File" or remove entirely.

## MEDIUM SEVERITY ISSUES

### 3. **Structure/Function Claims Beyond Cosmetic Scope**
**Problematic Language:**
- "trigger collagen and elastin production"
- "Rejuvenate skin naturally"
- "faster-healing skin"

**Issue:** Even cosmetics cannot claim to affect skin structure or healing processes.

**Recommended Fix:** Limit to appearance claims only: "may improve skin appearance" or "supports skin's natural look."

### 4. **Clinical Outcome Claims Without Substantiation**
**Problematic Language:**
- "Clinics report faster recovery and better results with fewer sessions"
- "Trusted Worldwide by 200+ Premium Clinics"

**Issue:** Clinical outcome claims require clinical evidence. FTC requires substantiation for numerical claims.

**Risk:** FTC enforcement for unsubstantiated claims.

**Recommended Fix:** Provide clinical data or remove claims. For clinic numbers, ensure documentation exists.

### 5. **"Clinical-Grade" Terminology**
**Problematic Language:**
- "clinical-grade, GMP products"
- "engineered for clinical and research excellence"

**Issue:** "Clinical-grade" implies medical use, contradicting cosmetic/research classification.

**Recommended Fix:** Use "laboratory-grade" or "research-grade" instead.

## LOW SEVERITY ISSUES

### 6. **Gating Strategy Concerns**
**Issue:** "Health and Wellness Professionals only" gating suggests medical use but may not provide adequate regulatory protection.

**Recommended Fix:** Clearly define intended professional users and ensure all marketing aligns with stated use.
